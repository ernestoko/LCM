# Product

## Register

product

> Dual-surface platform. The **app** (authenticated operations tooling) is the
> center of gravity and sets the default register: `product`. The **public
> marketing site** (`/`, services, coverage, about, FAQ, contact, legal) operates
> in the **brand** register — design is the product there. The shared design
> system must serve both: trustworthy and legible in the app, distinctive and
> confident on the marketing site.

## Users

**External (marketing site, tracking, registration):** traders, online sellers,
importers/exporters, families sending goods, and institutions shipping to and
from the USA, Ghana, China and across Africa. Context: deciding whether to trust
a cross-border shipper with valuable cargo, getting a transparent quote, or
checking where a package is right now. Often on mobile, sometimes on slow
connections, frequently first-time visitors with no prior relationship.

**Internal (app / dashboard):** Liberty super admins and admins, finance users,
SEAL partner-org admins and staff, and warehouse operators. Context: in a task —
intaking packages, generating invoices, confirming payments, building manifests,
reconciling settlements, running reports. Repeat daily users who value speed,
density, and consistency over novelty.

## Product Purpose

Liberty & Liberty Logistics is an end-to-end global-logistics platform: it wins
customers on the public site (quotes, tracking, trust), then runs the entire
shipment lifecycle in the app — intake, pricing, invoicing, payments, manifests,
delivery, and financial reconciliation across two partner organizations. Success
is a customer who trusts the brand enough to ship, and staff who can move a
shipment from door to door without friction or error.

## Brand Personality

**Trusted & dependable.** Calm, solid, institutional confidence — the feeling of
a carrier that has moved cargo for decades and will not lose yours. Voice is
plain, direct, and reassuring; never hypey. Three words: **steadfast, precise,
human.** The interface should lower a shipper's anxiety, not raise their pulse.

## Anti-references

- **Cheap courier site** — low-trust, stocky, bargain-bin, clip-art logistics.
- **Cluttered enterprise portal** — dense, dated, gray-on-gray corporate ERP.
- **Generic SaaS template** — a tracked uppercase eyebrow above every section,
  endless identical icon-cards, gradient text, hero-metric scaffolding.
- **Flashy startup** — loud gradients/neon, over-animation, growth-hack hype.

## Design Principles

1. **Legibility is trust.** Every number, status, and label must be unambiguous
   and high-contrast. A shipper reading a tracking status and a clerk reading an
   invoice both need zero doubt. No light-gray body text for "elegance".
2. **Earn confidence, don't perform it.** Substance over decoration — real
   imagery, real data, plain copy. Distinctiveness comes from craft and
   restraint, not effects.
3. **One system, two voices.** Shared tokens and primitives; the marketing site
   may speak louder and the app quieter, but they are recognizably one brand.
4. **The tool disappears in the app.** Familiar affordances, complete state
   vocabulary, consistent component grammar screen to screen. Delight lives in
   moments, not on every page.
5. **Calm motion.** Movement conveys state and guides the eye; it never gates
   content or shows off. Reduced-motion is a first-class path, not an afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: body text ≥4.5:1, large/secondary text ≥3:1, visible
focus rings on every interactive element, full keyboard operability, and
`prefers-reduced-motion` honored everywhere. Status must never rely on color
alone (pair with label/icon) — critical for a status-driven logistics workflow
and for color-blind users. Respect low-bandwidth visitors (optimized imagery,
content visible without JS).
